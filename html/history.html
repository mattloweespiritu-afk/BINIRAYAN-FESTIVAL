History of the Festival

The Story Behind Binirayan

A heritage-centered journey through memory, migration, legend, and the cultural identity of Antique.

Historical Background

From Legend to Cultural Memory

The history remembered through Binirayan lives at the meeting point of legend, oral tradition, and regional cultural memory. For Antiqueños, the story of the Datus is not only about origins, but also about identity, migration, resilience, and the meaning of belonging.

Chapter 1

The Arrival of the Datus

Local tradition tells of the Ten Bornean Datus who fled tyranny in search of freedom. They sailed across the seas in balangay boats, carrying with them leadership, memory, and the hope of beginning anew in a place where they could live with dignity.

Their remembered landing at Malandog in Hamtic became one of the central stories in Antique's historical imagination. Over time, this narrative became deeply woven into community identity and later into the symbolic meaning of the Binirayan Festival itself.

Chapter 2

The Maragtas Tradition

The Binirayan story is often linked to the Maragtas tradition, which recounts the encounter between the arriving Datus and the Ati people led in legend by Marikudo and Maniwantiwan. In this tradition, the story of settlement, exchange, and coexistence became part of the remembered beginnings of Panay communities.

In today's cultural context, this story is presented respectfully as part of Antique's remembered heritage. It is important to understand that Maragtas is widely treated as a compilation of legends and oral history, and the festival draws on this remembered tradition as a source of cultural meaning rather than presenting it as simple uncontested historical fact.

Cultural Note

Legend, Tradition, and Memory

The story of the Ten Bornean Datus remains deeply embedded in the cultural memory of Panay and Antique. For many communities, it continues to function as a symbolic origin story that expresses ideas of migration, leadership, settlement, and identity.

In this website, the story is presented respectfully as part of Antique's heritage and festival tradition. Binirayan celebrates this narrative not only as a story of the past, but also as a living source of identity, pride, and continuity for the province.

Chapter 3

The Establishment of Binirayan Festival

The modern Binirayan Festival was established in 1974 under the leadership of Governor Evelio B. Javier. It was conceived as a cultural celebration that would strengthen Antiqueño pride, honor remembered Malay roots, and provide the province with a unifying festival of heritage and identity.

Over time, the festival evolved into Antique's premier cultural event, featuring reenactments, ceremonies, parades, performances, and community participation. Its dates changed across different administrations before eventually being celebrated again in December, but its symbolic purpose remained closely tied to the province's effort to preserve memory and celebrate local culture.

Today, Binirayan continues to function not only as a historical commemoration, but also as a public expression of unity, tourism, and provincial identity.

Enduring Legacy
